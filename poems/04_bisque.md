---
title: "Bisque"
---

After the wheel, after the wire
cuts the piece free of the bat,
comes the waiting —

three days on the shelf
to dry. You cannot rush this.
The clay is letting go of water,

molecule by molecule,
shrinking toward itself,
becoming what it wants to be.

Touch it too soon and it warps.
This is called greenware:
bone-dry but fragile,

holding its shape on faith
before the first fire
transforms it into something real —

bisque: porous, pale, provisional,
ready to accept the glaze
that will become its skin.

I think of all the stages
a thing must pass through
before it can hold water.
