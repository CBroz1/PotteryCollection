---
title: "The Kiln"
---

Inside the kiln, at cone six,
the silica in the clay softens
and the glaze melts flat —
chemistry becoming art
at two thousand two hundred degrees.

We cannot watch.
We load the shelves and brick the door
and walk away for twelve hours,
trusting the elements
to do what elements do.

Every firing is a mystery.
The copper green can shift to red
depending on the oxygen,
the placement, the luck
of proximity to the burner.

My teacher lost a whole kiln once
to a bad thermocouple.
She opened the door to a graveyard
of cracked, bloated forms,
the glaze pooled on the shelf.

She photographed each piece.
She called it the firing's own record,
its insistence on being
something she hadn't planned.
Some of those pieces she kept.
