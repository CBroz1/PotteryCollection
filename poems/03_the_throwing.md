---
title: "The Throwing"
---

One hand inside, one out —
the wall rises between them
like a secret being told
in two directions at once.

I pull the clay upward
and it obeys, mostly.
Sometimes it tilts, torques,
throws itself back into a mound —
starts over without apology.

In the studio we call this
a collapse, but I've come to think
it is the clay proposing something else,
a different height, a different form,
a suggestion I was too stubborn to hear.

So I listen now with wet hands.
I let the walls thin
until they're nearly translucent
in the gray afternoon light —
thin enough to hold water,
thin enough to let the warmth
of a drink pass through to the palm.

This is what I'm after:
that moment when a cup
becomes a thing that holds
what people need.
