---
title: "The Collection"
---

On the shelf they stand together,
each one a particular decision —
this rim, this foot, this turning
of the handle at an angle
that fits the grip
of a specific hand.

None of them are the same.
The wheel sees to that.
The kiln sees to that.
I see to that,
pulling each one toward
some idea I can't quite name
and then letting it be
what it becomes instead.

A collection is not a series.
A collection is a conversation —
each piece answering the ones
that came before it,
each one a better question
about what the clay was trying to say
when it first came up
through the ground
and into my hands.

I don't know who will use these.
I don't know whose table
they will sit on,
whose morning coffee
they will hold,
whose hands will wrap around them
in the dark before the day begins.

But I made them
to be used,
to be held,
to wear the small stains
of being lived with —
and to last,
if they last,
as evidence
that someone stood here
and tried to make
something worth keeping.
