---
title: "Centering"
---

The wheel insists on wobble.
Everything you bring to it
will try to fly apart —
that is the nature of spinning.

The trick is not to grip,
but to lean in
with the heels of both hands
until the mass finds its own middle,
until resistance smoothes
into something you can trust.

My teacher said: don't fight the clay,
become its axis.
I didn't understand her then.
I stood above it, pressing down,
watching my knuckles whiten.

Now I know the center isn't found —
it's made, and only
when you stop trying
to impose it.
The clay teaches this.
The clay is patient.
