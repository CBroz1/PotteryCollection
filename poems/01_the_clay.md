---
title: "The Clay"
---

Before it was a bowl, it was a hillside.
Before it was a hillside, it was the sea —
compressed through ages into something
that yields, that holds a thumbprint
like a confidence.

I wedge it now against the table,
kneading out the air,
those tiny voids that would become
catastrophe inside the kiln —
small silences that crack.

What I love most is this:
that clay remembers nothing
and everything. Press a shell into it
and the shape stays
long after you have walked away.

This is why we make things from the earth —
not to last, though some do last,
but to practice the gesture
of pressing something permanent
into something that will change.
